Sun Protection

Australia is known for its higher average UV index compared to many other regions, underscoring the importance of sun protection measures even at relatively lower UV thresholds, such as 6-7. The emphasis on protective clothing and sunscreen usage aligns closely with the heightened sun exposure typically experienced in Australia.

Sun protection is essential to prevent skin damage and reduce the risk of skin cancer. Exposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun can cause sunburn, premature aging, and skin cancer.

Wearing protective clothing and using sunscreen helps to block harmful UV rays and keep your skin safe. Don't forget to reapply sunscreen every two hours, especially after swimming or sweating, and seek shade during peak sun hours to further protect your skin.
