Data about UV impacts

UV penetration into the layers of the skin

The Sun's ultraviolet (UV) radiation is classified into three categories according to wavelength:

- UVA

- UVB

- UVC

UVA radiation possesses the greatest wavelength and is linked to the process of skin aging, whereas UVB radiation possesses a shorter wavelength and is related to skin burning. The Earth's ozone layer predominantly absorbs UVC radiation, preventing it from reaching the Earth's surface. Extended exposure to UVA and UVB radiation can result in multiple dermatological conditions, such as sunburn, premature skin aging, and perhaps skin cancer. It is crucial to shield your skin from UV rays throughout the year, not only in the summer or when you are at the beach.
